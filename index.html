Paper Airplace Tutorial

Materials needed:

  • A standard letter-size (8.5 x 11 inches) sheet of paper

Instructions:

  1. Start with the paper oriented in a landscape (horizontal) position.
  2. Fold the paper in half lengthwise. Align the edges carefully to create a sharp crease.
  3. Unfold the paper. You should now see a crease line down the center of the paper.
  4. Fold the top edges of the paper down to meet the center crease. This creates a triangular shape at the top.
  5. Fold the two new triangular flaps you just created so that their edges meet at the center crease.
  6. Fold the entire top section (the two triangles and a bit of the base) down along the original center crease. This will be the wings of your paper airplane.
  7. Fold the wings down so that their top edges align with the bottom edge of the plane. Flip the paper over.
  8. Fold the two top corners inwards so that they meet at the center line, creating a point at the front of the airplane.
  9. SFold the entire plane in half along the original center crease. This brings the wings down and forms the body of the airplane.
  10. Adjust the wings to your liking. You may want to experiment with the wing angle for different flying characteristics.
Your paper airplane is now ready to take flight! Adjustments to the folds can be made to optimize performance, so feel free to experiment and have fun with it.
